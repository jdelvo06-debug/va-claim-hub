# What Is TDIU? Total Disability Based on Individual Unemployability

Total Disability based on Individual Unemployability — commonly called **TDIU** or **IU** — is one of the most powerful benefits available to veterans. It allows the VA to pay you at the **100% disability rate** even if your combined rating is below 100%, as long as your service-connected conditions prevent you from maintaining substantially gainful employment.

For many veterans, TDIU is the path to 100% compensation when their rating percentage does not yet reflect the full economic impact of their disabilities.

## The Basic Concept

The VA uses the VASRD (VA Schedule for Rating Disabilities) to assign percentage ratings based on medical criteria. But a rating percentage does not always capture whether a veteran can actually work. A veteran rated at 70% for PTSD may be completely unable to hold a job — and TDIU bridges that gap.

Under 38 CFR 4.16, if your service-connected disabilities prevent you from securing and following a "substantially gainful occupation," you may qualify for TDIU regardless of your combined rating percentage.

## Eligibility Requirements

### Schedular TDIU (38 CFR 4.16(a))

The standard path to TDIU requires meeting a **rating threshold**:

- **Single disability rated at 60% or more**, OR
- **Two or more disabilities with a combined rating of 70% or more**, where at least one individual disability is rated at 40% or more

These are not hard cutoffs for being considered unemployable — they are the thresholds for the "schedular" TDIU path, which is the faster administrative route.

**Important:** A "single disability" for purposes of the 60% threshold can include disabilities that are considered together under specific circumstances, such as bilateral conditions or disabilities affecting the same body system.

### Extraschedular TDIU (38 CFR 4.16(b))

If you do not meet the schedular rating thresholds but are still unable to work due to service-connected conditions, you can be referred for **extraschedular TDIU**. This requires approval from VA Central Office and is harder to obtain, but it exists specifically for veterans whose disabilities are exceptionally disabling relative to their rating percentage.

## What "Substantially Gainful Employment" Means

The VA defines substantially gainful employment as employment that pays above the **federal poverty threshold**. If you can only work sporadically, in a sheltered environment (like a family business that accommodates your disabilities), or in a marginal job significantly below your education and work history, that is generally not "substantially gainful."

**Key factors the VA considers:**
- Your service-connected disabilities (not non-service-connected conditions)
- Work history and education level (a veteran who worked as a manual laborer may have different functional requirements than a veteran who worked in an office)
- Ability to maintain regular attendance and complete a normal workday/workweek
- Whether you have been fired from jobs due to your disabilities

## How to File for TDIU

### VA Form 21-8940

TDIU is filed using **VA Form 21-8940**, "Veteran's Application for Increased Compensation Based on Unemployability." This form asks about:

- Your employment history for the past five years
- The name and contact information of your last employer
- Whether your disabilities have caused you to miss work or change jobs
- Your education and vocational training

Filing Form 21-8940 is the formal trigger for the TDIU claim. The VA is also required to consider TDIU when the evidence in your file reasonably raises the issue — even if you have not filed the form. However, filing proactively is always recommended.

### Supporting Evidence

Strong TDIU claims include:

**Medical evidence of functional limitations:**
A physician letter (or nexus-style opinion) that specifically addresses your *ability to work* — not just your diagnosis. The opinion should describe how your service-connected conditions cause attendance problems, inability to concentrate, physical limitations, or other barriers to employment.

**Employment records:**
Termination letters, attendance records, and employer statements showing that your disabilities caused job loss or inability to maintain employment.

**VA vocational rehabilitation assessment:**
If you have been evaluated by VA vocational rehabilitation and found not feasible for employment, that assessment is powerful evidence.

**Social Security Disability records:**
If Social Security has determined you are disabled (SSDI), that determination is relevant evidence. The VA is not bound by it, but it supports your TDIU claim.

## TDIU and the 100% Rate

TDIU pays the same monthly compensation as a schedular 100% rating. As of 2026, that is **$3,831.30/month for a single veteran** (with additional amounts for dependents). This is substantially higher than a 70% or 80% combined rating.

TDIU is treated as "total disability" for most VA benefit purposes, including:
- VA health care (Priority Group 1)
- Dependency and Indemnity Compensation (DIC) eligibility for survivors
- Vocational Rehabilitation & Employment eligibility
- Chapter 35 dependents' educational assistance

## TDIU and Marginal Employment

Veterans receiving TDIU *can* work in some circumstances — specifically **marginal employment**. If your annual earned income does not exceed the federal poverty threshold for a single person (approximately $15,000 in 2026), the VA generally considers this marginal and will not terminate TDIU.

Employment in a **protected work environment** (a business owned by a family member, for example) where your employer accommodates your disabilities in ways that would not be available in a competitive job market also does not terminate TDIU.

## Protecting Your TDIU

The VA can propose to reduce or terminate TDIU if they determine you have been employed above the marginal threshold. If you receive a proposed reduction:

1. Respond within **60 days** requesting a predetermination hearing
2. Submit medical evidence documenting that your condition has not improved
3. Consider working with an accredited VA attorney

Annual employment questionnaires (VA Form 21-4140) are sent to TDIU recipients. Complete and return these promptly. Failure to respond can trigger a proposed reduction.

## The Bottom Line

TDIU is available to any veteran whose service-connected disabilities prevent them from working a substantially gainful job — regardless of whether their rating percentage is at 100%. If you are unemployed or marginally employed because of your service-connected conditions, file VA Form 21-8940 and support it with medical and employment evidence showing how your disabilities specifically prevent you from working.
