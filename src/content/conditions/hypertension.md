# Hypertension VA Disability Rating Guide

Hypertension is rated under Diagnostic Code 7101. While the individual rating for hypertension tends to be modest, it adds to your combined rating and opens the door for secondary claims related to cardiovascular complications.

## Rating Criteria

**0%** — Diagnosed hypertension with diastolic pressure predominantly less than 100, or systolic pressure predominantly less than 160. Requires continuous medication for control.

**10%** — Diastolic pressure predominantly 100 or more, OR systolic pressure predominantly 160 or more, OR individual with a history of diastolic pressure predominantly 100 or more who requires continuous medication for control.

**20%** — Diastolic pressure predominantly 110 or more, or systolic pressure predominantly 200 or more.

**40%** — Diastolic pressure predominantly 120 or more.

**60%** — Diastolic pressure predominantly 130 or more.

## Secondary Connections

Hypertension is commonly claimed secondary to:

- **Sleep apnea** — Recurrent oxygen desaturation and sympathetic nervous system activation during apneic episodes directly contribute to sustained hypertension. This connection has strong medical literature support.
- **PTSD** — Chronic hyperarousal elevates cortisol and adrenaline levels, contributing to sustained blood pressure elevation.
- **Kidney disease** — Service-connected renal conditions can cause hypertension.
- **Agent Orange exposure** — Hypertension was added to the presumptive conditions list for herbicide-exposed veterans.

## Key Evidence

- Blood pressure readings over time (a log of daily readings is helpful)
- Prescription records for antihypertensive medications
- Documentation of the onset of hypertension in relation to your primary service-connected condition
- Nexus letter if claiming secondary

## Filing Tips

Even a 0% service-connected rating for hypertension is worth pursuing — it establishes the connection and allows future increases as the condition worsens. It also enables secondary claims for any cardiovascular complications that develop.
