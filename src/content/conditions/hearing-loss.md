# Hearing Loss VA Disability Rating Guide

Sensorineural hearing loss from noise exposure is widespread among veterans, yet it often results in frustratingly low ratings — even 0% — due to how the VA's rating tables work. Understanding the mechanics can help you maximize your claim.

## How the VA Rates Hearing Loss

Hearing loss is rated under Diagnostic Codes 6100-6110 using a **mechanical formula** based on two measurements from a VA audiology exam:

1. **Puretone threshold average** — the average of your hearing thresholds at 1000, 2000, 3000, and 4000 Hz in each ear
2. **Speech discrimination score** — using the Maryland CNC word list

These two values for each ear are plotted on **Table VI** (or Table VIa for exceptional patterns) to produce a Roman numeral designation (I through XI) for each ear. The two Roman numerals are then cross-referenced on **Table VII** to determine your disability percentage.

### Why Ratings Are Often Low

The VA formula is weighted heavily toward higher frequencies and requires significant hearing loss before assigning ratings above 0%. A veteran can have noticeable real-world hearing difficulty and still receive a 0% rating because the audiometric scores do not meet the table thresholds.

A **0% service-connected rating** is still valuable — it establishes service connection, preserves eligibility for VA-provided hearing aids, and allows the rating to increase if hearing worsens over time.

## Exceptional Patterns

You may qualify for rating under Table VIa (which can produce a higher Roman numeral) if:
- Puretone threshold at each of the four frequencies (1000, 2000, 3000, 4000 Hz) is 55 decibels or greater, OR
- Puretone threshold is 30 dB or less at 1000 Hz and 70 dB or more at 2000 Hz

## Evidence Needed

- Entry and separation audiograms showing a threshold shift during service
- MOS establishing hazardous noise exposure
- Current audiogram and speech discrimination test from a licensed audiologist
- Buddy statements about difficulty hearing in conversation, needing TV volume raised, or asking people to repeat themselves

## C&P Exam Tips

- The exam is objective — the audiometer measures your hearing. Do not try to exaggerate; the test has built-in reliability checks.
- Ensure speech discrimination is tested using the **Maryland CNC** word list (not another test). This is a regulatory requirement.
- If you have difficulty understanding speech in noisy environments but perform well in the quiet booth, mention this to the examiner for inclusion in the report.

## Filing Strategy

Always file hearing loss alongside [tinnitus](/conditions/tinnitus). Even if hearing loss rates at 0%, tinnitus provides an additional 10% rating. If hearing worsens, file for an increase — the VA must re-evaluate using current audiometric data.
