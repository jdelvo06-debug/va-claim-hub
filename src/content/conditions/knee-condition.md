# Knee Conditions VA Disability Rating Guide

Knee injuries and degenerative conditions are extremely common among veterans, particularly those in infantry, airborne, and other physically demanding specialties. The VA rates knee conditions under several diagnostic codes, and veterans can often receive **separate ratings** for different aspects of the same knee.

## VA Knee Rating Codes

### Limitation of Flexion (DC 5260)
- **0%** — Flexion limited to 60 degrees
- **10%** — Flexion limited to 45 degrees
- **20%** — Flexion limited to 30 degrees
- **30%** — Flexion limited to 15 degrees

### Limitation of Extension (DC 5261)
- **0%** — Extension limited to 5 degrees
- **10%** — Extension limited to 10 degrees
- **20%** — Extension limited to 15 degrees
- **30%** — Extension limited to 20 degrees
- **40%** — Extension limited to 30 degrees
- **50%** — Extension limited to 45 degrees

### Instability/Subluxation (DC 5257)
- **10%** — Slight recurrent subluxation or lateral instability
- **20%** — Moderate instability
- **30%** — Severe instability

### Important: You can receive separate ratings for instability AND limited motion in the same knee under VAOPGCPREC 23-97. This is called "pyramiding" exception and can significantly increase your combined rating.

## Key Evidence

- Range of motion measurements (active, passive, weight-bearing, non-weight-bearing per *Correia*)
- MRI results showing meniscal tears, ligament damage, or cartilage degeneration
- Documented instability via clinical testing (Lachman test, anterior drawer, varus/valgus stress)
- Functional impact statements describing difficulty with stairs, walking distances, standing, or kneeling

## C&P Exam Tips

- Ensure the examiner tests for instability separately from range of motion
- Report pain on motion — the examiner must document the degree where pain begins
- Describe flare-up frequency and additional limitation during flare-ups
- Mention any locking, giving way, or clicking of the knee joint

## Secondary Conditions

Knee conditions commonly lead to secondary claims for [lumbar strain](/conditions/lumbar-strain) (from altered gait), opposite knee strain (from compensating), hip conditions, and [depression](/conditions/depression) from chronic pain and limited mobility.
