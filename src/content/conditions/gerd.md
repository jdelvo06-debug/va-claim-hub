# GERD VA Disability Rating Guide

Gastroesophageal reflux disease is common among veterans and often develops secondary to medications (NSAIDs for pain conditions), PTSD-related stress, or as a direct result of military dietary conditions and stress. GERD is rated under the digestive system codes and can contribute meaningfully to a combined rating.

## How the VA Rates GERD

GERD does not have its own specific diagnostic code. It is typically rated by analogy under **Diagnostic Code 7346** (hiatal hernia), which provides the closest matching symptom criteria:

**10%** — Two or more of the following: recurrent epigastric distress, dysphagia (difficulty swallowing), pyrosis (heartburn), regurgitation, substernal or arm or shoulder pain. These symptoms are productive of considerable impairment of health.

**30%** — Persistently recurrent epigastric distress with dysphagia, pyrosis, and regurgitation, accompanied by substernal or arm or shoulder pain, productive of considerable impairment of health.

**60%** — Symptoms of pain, vomiting, material weight loss and hematemesis or melena with moderate anemia, or other symptom combinations productive of severe impairment of health.

## Secondary Service Connection

GERD is most commonly claimed secondary to:

- **PTSD and anxiety** — The gut-brain axis is well-established in medical literature. Chronic stress increases stomach acid production and disrupts esophageal sphincter function.
- **NSAID medications** prescribed for service-connected pain conditions (back, knee, shoulder injuries). Long-term NSAID use is a recognized cause of gastric and esophageal damage.
- **Sleep apnea** — Increased intra-abdominal pressure during apneic episodes can worsen reflux.

## Evidence Tips

- Prescription records for acid-reducing medications (omeprazole, pantoprazole, ranitidine)
- Endoscopy or barium swallow results if available
- Documentation of dietary restrictions imposed by the condition
- Weight changes if applicable
- Nexus letter connecting GERD to your service-connected conditions

## C&P Exam Preparation

- Describe symptom frequency and severity in specific terms: "I experience reflux 4-5 days per week, requiring daily medication, and I've had to eliminate multiple foods from my diet."
- Explain how GERD affects your sleep, diet, weight, and overall quality of life
- Mention any complications such as Barrett's esophagus, esophageal stricture, or chronic cough from aspiration
